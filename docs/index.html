How it actually works

Grounded, not guessed.

Most translation apps ask a language model to just produce an answer. Soutuk gives that model real reference material first.

DOT-grounded translation

Every language pair is backed by real per-language grammar cards — word order, case systems, phoneme hazards, honorific and evidentiality rules — sourced from linguistic typology research (WALS), not just what a model happened to learn during training.

Safety gating

High-stakes medical and legal terms are flagged automatically. Silent mistranslation in those domains isn't treated as an acceptable risk — the app surfaces uncertainty instead of hiding it.

Dual-isolate arbitration

For ancient and low-resource languages where a single model is unreliable, two independent translation attempts are cross-checked against each other before you see an answer.

Conversation Mode

A real two-person interpreter flow: introduce yourself, and Soutuk identifies the other person's language automatically — no manual picker required for them.

Language coverage

Real coverage, honestly counted.

1189 languages with grammar scaffolding

Soutuk builds a structured grammar profile — word order, case systems, phoneme hazards, honorific rules — for each language from real typology research (WALS), before any translation happens. This scaffolding is generated by a machine-learning research pipeline, not hand-curated by linguists. A language only appears in Soutuk once it has real scaffolding behind it — not a generic fallback dressed up as support. Coverage grows from an ongoing research pipeline, not a one-time dataset.
